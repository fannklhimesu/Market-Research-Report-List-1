<p><p>Gaming motherboards come in different market types, primarily BTX and ATX. The ATX type is the most common, offering a standard layout with ample expansion slots and good airflow, catering to high-performance gaming setups. BTX, while less prevalent, features a design aimed at enhancing thermal efficiency, positioning components for better heat dissipation, which can be beneficial in overclocking scenarios. Both formats cater to gamers, but ATX remains dominant due to its versatility and widespread compatibility with gaming hardware.</p></p>
